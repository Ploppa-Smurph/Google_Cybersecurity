    02) Explore the CISSP Security Domains, Part 1
           -Security Posture- organization's ability to manage defense of critical assets, and react to change

            01) Security and Risk Management: Define security goals and objectives; Risk mitigation; Compliance; Business continuity; Legal regulations
                    - Risk Mitigation: having procedures and controls in place to reduce impact of risk
                    - Compliance:
                    - Business Continuity: organization's ability to maintain everyday productivity by establishing risk disaster recovery plans
            02) Asset Security: securing digital and physical assets. Relates to: storage, maintainance, retention, and destruction of data 
            03) Security Architecture and Engineering: optimize data security with tools, systems, and processes in place to protect assets and data
                    - Shared Responsibilty: ALL individuals within organization take an active role in lowering risk and maintaining both physical and virtual security
            04) Communication and Network Security: managing and securing physical networks and wireless communication

    03) Explore the CISSP Security Domains, Part 2
            05) Identity and Access Management (IAM): access and authorization to data should be secure & users MUST follow established policies to control and manage assets
                    - entry level analysts help make certain people have correct access and authorization -- 
                    4 componetns of IAM: Identification, Authentication, Authorization, Accountability
            06) Security Assessment and Testing: conducting security testing, collecting and analyzing data, and conducting security audits for risks, threats, and vulnerabilities 
            07) Security Operations: conducting investigations and implementing preventitive measures
            08) Software Development Security: using secure coding practices while developing applications
